Pre-Lift Planning & Engineering

Complex lifts require more than capacity—they demand engineering analysis and detailed planning. For projects involving critical lifts, unusual configurations, or challenging conditions, we provide comprehensive pre-lift planning services:

Load Analysis: We calculate actual load weights (including rigging), determine center of gravity, account for dynamic loads during lifting, and verify crane capacity at required radius with appropriate safety factors.

Site Assessment: Our team evaluates ground conditions and load-bearing capacity, identifies overhead obstructions (power lines, structures), determines crane positioning and outrigger placement, and verifies adequate clearances for boom movement.

Lift Plan Development: We create documented lift plans showing crane placement, load path, rigging configuration, critical radii and boom angles, and emergency procedures. For critical lifts, we conduct pre-lift meetings with all personnel.

Permits & Notifications: We handle crane operating permits where required, coordinate utility notifications for work near power lines, arrange for road closures or lane restrictions if needed, and ensure all regulatory requirements are met.

Safety: Our Core Value

Crane operations involve substantial risks—overhead loads, complex rigging, proximity to workers and structures. We address these risks through systematic safety protocols:

Equipment Inspection: Every crane undergoes daily pre-operational inspection per OSHA requirements, monthly detailed inspections documented in maintenance logs, annual third-party certification, and immediate removal from service if deficiencies are identified.

Operational Procedures: We establish and maintain exclusion zones around operating cranes, implement tag line control for load stability, use spotters for obstructed views or tight clearances, maintain constant communication via hand signals or two-way radio, and monitor wind speeds with immediate shutdown if limits are exceeded.

Site Safety: Before beginning operations, we identify and barricade overhead power lines, verify ground conditions can support crane outriggers, establish clear communication protocols with site personnel, conduct safety briefings with all workers in the area, and maintain emergency response plans.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know what size crane I need?
A: Our estimators help determine appropriate crane size based on load weight, lift height, and required radius. Generally, heavier loads, higher lifts, and greater distances from crane require larger capacity. We'll review your project and recommend the right crane.

Q: Can you operate near power lines?
A: Yes, with proper precautions. OSHA requires minimum clearance distances from energized lines (typically 20 feet for lines up to 50kV). For closer work, we coordinate with utilities to de-energize lines or install protective barriers. Power line safety is a critical part of our pre-job planning.

Q: What ground conditions can support crane operations?
A: Most cranes can operate on reasonably firm, level ground. We use outrigger pads to distribute loads and can work on asphalt, concrete, compacted gravel, or stable soil. Soft ground, steep slopes, or underground utilities may require special preparations like timber mats or engineered pads.

Q: Do you provide rigging as part of crane service?
A: Yes. Operated crane service includes standard rigging hardware (slings, shackles, hooks) appropriate for the lift. Specialized rigging (spreader bars, custom attachments) is available and priced separately when needed.

Q: How far in advance should we schedule crane service?
A: For routine lifts, 1-2 weeks notice ensures equipment availability. For specific crane types or peak construction season, 2-4 weeks is better. Emergency response is available 24/7 with typical mobilization within 4-8 hours.
